MESOSCALE MODELING OF BIOMOLECULES

In order to edit a supramolecular structure composed of multiple biopolymers with computer software, the polygon mesh model is the useful modeling object describing shape of molecules with a size of several nano maters. We may also expect the rigid body dynamics calculation can be applied using general-purpose 3D CG software. Here, our example of using Blender, a 3D CG animation software is demonstrated. Please note the illustrated motions are not determined experimentaly by biologist, but are predictions. They will be tools for understanding biological discussions. Blender is free software, and we are developing numbers of script file for Blender. Please download these scripts to modify and exchange the model, so that we might help biologist to construct biologically correct animations based on structural studies for proteins and biological macromoleculesby exchanging and improving Blender scripts.

MODELING FOR STRUCTURAL STUDIES OF ARTIFICIAL MUSCLES

The artificial muscle reported by Hokkaido University and Kansai University demonstrated a possible actuator material using microtubules and kinesin. Fluorescent molecules have been introduced into the microtubules to facilitate observation with an optical microscope. In order to construct a detailed 3D model a conforcal laser scanning microscope (CLSM) was used at a resolution approaching super resolution. The arrangement of microtubules, which is a cylindrical structure, was visualized, and we attempted to construct a three-dimensional model of microtubules.

　
　
A mesh model by cylinders of microtubule that fits the three-dimensional volume of the observed microtubule are constructed. The three-dimensional volume data are convert the iso-surface polygon mesh model by ImageJ software. Then, the cylinder objects are fitted into the volume using Blender. Two areas, a dense area and a surface area that represents the overall volume, were created and saved with export STL.

To find a nice fit of cylinder models for the observed iso-surface mesh object a semi-transparent display was useful overlaying the structure. As a result, many filaments had a length of about 4 µm, and filaments of about 7.5 µm were observed. The size will differ depending on the condition of the specimen.

CONSTRUCTION OF SKELETAL MUSCLE FILAMENT MODEL

The muscle filaments that make up skeletal muscle are arranged in a hexagonal lattice, and are known to have a unique arrangement in vertebrates. Further, when the muscle contracts, the lattice spacing becomes shorter corresponding to the extension of the muscle cell. Here, we attempted to confirm the intermolecular interaction between actin and myosin on the model by reconstructing the structure of the filament lattice, which was not dealt with in the conventional sarcomeric shortening model. Facilitated the creation of a model that was also contracted. I tried to utilize the particle function of Blender. In order to nest two particle models, one is a particle model with leaves on the branches of the tree, and the other is a particle model of the trunk with multiple branches. Apply the particle instance modifier to the upper part of the branches, and use the Use Modifier in the particle panel. Turn on Stack. The total number of myosin heads generated from the branches is the total number.

PREDICTION OF SMOOTH MUSCLE FILAMENT MODEL

Since smooth muscle does not have a sarcomere structure, its detailed structure is not so clear, but a hypothesis has been proposed for the contraction mechanism. We tried to create a model based on the hypothesis that the unit structure of contraction is composed of actin from the textbook explanation. We created a state in which this unit structure constitutes a network connected to a mesh centered on a mass known as a dens body. And it was illustrated that the contraction of the whole network is given by shortening the unit structure. I created a unit structure with the Group function of Blender, created a mesh structure with armature so that it could be given an action, and pasted multiple unit structures registered with Group. Since the myosin filament was created in the same manner as the skeletal muscle, it has the same movement as the neighbor, but it is not synchronized, and it is expected that the sliding length of the filament is not fixed.
